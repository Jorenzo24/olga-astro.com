The wellbeing reading

What a medical astrology reading actually tells you

Every birth chart carries a quiet portrait of vitality: how your energy tends to rise and fall, where your constitution feels robust and where it asks for more care, the times you run on full and the times you need to slow down. A medical astrology reading reads that portrait gently. It weaves together the placements that traditional astrology has long linked with energy, temperament and resilience, and translates them into plain, supportive language about how you might look after yourself.

This is not the same as a health diagnosis, and it is important to be clear about that. A medical astrology reading does not name diseases, predict illness, prescribe anything, or promise cures. It is a reflective, self-awareness tool offered alongside, never instead of, professional medical care. If you have a health concern, please see a qualified doctor. What this reading offers is a softer, complementary lens: a way to understand your own rhythms of stress and rest, and to treat your wellbeing with a little more patience.

Most people come to a medical astrology reading with one honest wish underneath: I want to understand my own energy, and be kinder to it. A reading will not hand you a verdict about your body. It will show you the constitutional tendencies written into your chart, the seasons when your vitality naturally dips, and the simple, restorative habits that suit your nature, so that caring for yourself feels less like guesswork and more like coming home.

What your chart reveals

Your natural constitution

The underlying temperament of your chart: whether your energy tends to run warm and quick or steady and slow, and what that asks of you.

Where your vitality lives

The areas where your energy feels most alive and resilient, and the parts of you that tire more easily and want gentler handling.

Your rhythms of stress and rest

How pressure tends to build for you and how you truly recover, so you can work with your own tempo instead of against it.

Sensitive points to honour

The places where you may feel things more keenly, named softly, so you can give them attention and care rather than worry.

What restores you

The kinds of rest, movement and renewal that suit your nature, the simple habits that genuinely top up your energy.

The seasons of your energy

The cycles ahead that tend to lift or lower your vitality, so you know when to push forward and when to soften and recover.
