The vocation reading

What a career astrology reading actually tells you

Your birth chart is the map of the sky at the moment you were born, and within it certain places speak directly to work and direction. A career astrology reading studies them closely: the Midheaven and tenth house, which describe your public path and calling; Saturn, which shows where you build something lasting; the North Node, which points toward growth; and the talents written across the rest of the chart. Read together, they describe the shape of your vocation.

A generic "best careers for your Sun sign" list speaks to millions of people at once and means almost nothing. A career reading speaks only to you. It does not hand you a single job title or promise success. Instead it names, in plain language, the kind of work that fits how you are actually built, the environments where you do your best, and the directions your chart keeps leaning toward, so the choice in front of you stops feeling like a guess.

Most people come to a vocational astrology reading with one honest question underneath: is this really the work I'm meant to be doing? A reading will not decide your life for you. It will show you the real map of your calling, the talents you may be underusing, and the better windows for launches, moves and decisions, so the next step feels purposeful rather than merely busy.
