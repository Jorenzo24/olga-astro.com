The foundation reading

What birth time rectification actually does

Every birth chart is a map of the sky at the exact moment you were born. The planets in it move slowly, but the rising sign, the houses and the angles shift every few minutes. Those are what place each planet into a real area of life, and they set the timing on which forecasting depends. Without an accurate time, a chart is incomplete: the houses may be wrong, and any reading built on them loses its precision.

Many people simply do not have their exact time. It was never recorded, the memory is hazy, or the certificate only gives a rough hour. Birth time rectification solves this honestly. It is careful detective work: Olga takes datable events that really happened in your life and tests possible birth times against them, looking for the one time that explains them all most consistently. The chart stops being a guess and becomes a reliable foundation.

It is fair to be clear about what rectification is and is not. It is not magic, and it is not instant. It asks for your input, a timeline of significant dated events, and it gives you a best-supported time rather than absolute certainty. With a rich, accurate timeline, that result is precise enough to read the houses and angles with confidence, which is exactly what every other reading you go on to book will rest upon.
